About

Building things that bridge research and reality

I'm a machine learning engineer at Theta Vision, where I build annotation infrastructure, contribute to computer vision research, and help turn prototype models into production systems. My background spans embedded systems, clinical sensor algorithms, Test-Driven Development at Philips, and LLM fine-tuning — which means I tend to be most useful at the boundaries between disciplines.

I hold a Master's degree from Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e), where my thesis work in collaboration with ASML resulted in a published paper on deep learning for surface reconstruction from scanning electron microscope data. I graduated with an 8 out of 10.

Outside of work, I find it hard to stop thinking about it — which is probably a good sign.

Things I've built

Cortex — Annotation Infrastructure

Theta Vision · 2023–present

Platform

Developed a broad suite of annotation tooling within Cortex, Theta Vision's internal data management platform. This includes segmentation tooling with SAM 2.1 and SAM 3 assistance, bounding box and localization tools, panoptic segmentation, multi-object tracking (MOT) annotation, and video interval / timestamp annotation. Also contributed to architectural decisions and Django/React/TypeScript stack improvements across the platform.

Django React TypeScript SAM 2.1 / SAM 3 Computer Vision

IMAGEN — Animal Tracking & Behaviour Analysis

Theta Vision × Wageningen University · 2025–present

Research

Contributing to a project aimed at improving farm animal welfare through high-quality tracking and behavioural analysis in pen environments. Upgraded the ECAF tracking algorithm with a segmentation-based detector stage, developed an efficient video mask propagation tool for building annotated training datasets, and collaborated with intern Tijn Bergmans on training the updated pipeline. Two papers from this project have been accepted for publication.

Python PyTorch Multi-Object Tracking Segmentation

LLM Fine-tuning — Document Extraction & Summarisation

Theta Vision · 2023–2024

R&D

Led two separate LLM fine-tuning projects: one focused on automated extraction of structured data from trucking transit orders, and one aimed at generating plain-language video scripts from complex pharmaceutical package inserts (bijsluiters) for Healthbase. Fine-tuned Qwen and experimented with quantisation strategies and model sizes to balance performance and compute constraints. Both reached working prototypes; in the Healthbase case, the fine-tuned model measurably outperformed off-the-shelf alternatives.

Qwen Fine-tuning Quantisation Python

Master's Thesis — SEM Surface Reconstruction (ASML)

TU/e × ASML · 2022–2023

Academic

Graduation project supervised by PhD researcher Tim Houben, in collaboration with ASML. Developed a deep learning approach to optimise 3D surface reconstruction from segmented scanning electron microscope (SEM) detector data, addressing noise and geometric limitations inherent to SEM imaging. Graduated with an 8/10, resulting in a co-authored peer-reviewed publication.
